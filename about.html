Where do I come from?

Berkshire County Massachussetts

The Berkshires is the western most county in Mass. Depending on your reaons for visiting, the main
town or city could vary between North Adams, Great Barrington, Pittsdfield, or Stockbridge.

Pittsfield is likely going to be your go-to for a school, home to Berkshire Community College. There
is also a museum. Besides that, Berkshire Medical Center. The mall is all but closed.

North Adams is another college town, and arguablly the "artsy" region of the county. Home to MASS MoCA,
which is a another musem for contempoary art, the largest in the USA.

Stockbridge, historic, and famous, due to the Red Lion Inn and Norman Rockwell. Up the street is a,
renowed Catholic attraction, The Marian Father's, or the official name:The National Shrine of
the Divine Mercy. This is also the town that inspired the song, Alice's Restaurant.

Great Barrington. This is far from just a name. It indicates its historical signifigance. Had it NOT
been for an event in this town, in 1774, we may not have become the country we are today.
To save this from becoming a history journal, in short: we were the first organized resistance against British rule,
in thecolonies. We also performed a jail-break as well. Within a year, it was a wave that led
to the battle of Lexington & Concord in 1775. Essentially lighting the fuse for the Revolutionary War. There is
a stone commemorrating this outside of the town hall. We were also the first to extend
electrical power more than a mile. We have a picture of a horse and buggy at a stop light, as well. The Revolution
was also contingent for the Knox Expedition, taking a cannon from Fort
Ticonderoga all the way to Boston.

The town is also home to the bowling alley that helped inspire The Big Lebowski, the owner was also one of my
coaches, "Red." I was an avid bowler until I joined the adult league.

I grew up here, until I moved away in 2003. This was a fantastic area for art and history. We even have a castle.
But what really brings me home, is the job with John F. Graney. He fought me even working with him,
but with help and persistence, he relented and allwed me to work there. Honestly, I squandered the resources I had at my
fingertips. I learned, yes, but my teenage know-all attitude also prevented me from truley grasping
what I had. I now live in an area with an HOA, and am struggling to get my forge and equipment set up.

After leaving home, I moved to Maine, and then eventually joined the Army. Uncle Sam has plopped me here, in Kansas City.
I have ultimately turned to tech for a living.
